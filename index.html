Research

I’m interested in geospatial analysis, environmental risk modelling, and sustainable urban systems. My work focuses on inferring ecological dynamics such as flood patterns, terrain vulnerabilities, and human-environment interactions from spatial data using GIS tools like ArcGIS Pro and remote sensing. At YorkU, I map flood risks under Professor Gunho Sohn and collaborate with RealLife Robotics at YSpace to optimize autonomous delivery systems through geospatial problem solving. Proficient in Python, SQL, and GIS, I aim to transform spatial insights into strategies for resilient cities. A few of my recent projects are included below to highlight the range and depth of my work..

Integrated Flood Hazard Assessment of the Greater Toronto Area: Employing Stakeholder Perspectives using Fuzzy Functions and the Analytic Hierarchy Process
Anthony Loschiavo, Meetkumar Patel, William Ross
EU/GEOG 4340 3.00: Spatial Analysis & Problem Solving GIS, 2025
PDF / Data (Available on Request)

Developed a comprehensive flood hazard assessment for the GTA integrating Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) with stakeholder perspectives. Combined Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) weighting with fuzzy logic in GIS to create consensus-based flood risk maps. Analyzed 7 environmental factors across 6M+ population areas, incorporating inputs from municipal governments, conservation authorities, and residents.

Key features: Stakeholder-weighted risk modeling • Fuzzy function classification • 30m resolution spatial analysis • Composite hazard mapping • Climate change adaptation strategies

Remote Sensing Assessment of Phragmites Management along Tom Taylor Trail: Sentinel-2, LiDAR, Texture, and Multi-Index Analysis
William Ross
EU/GEOG 4440 M: Processing and Analysis of Earth Observation, 2025
PDF / Geospatial Data

Evaluated invasive Phragmites management effectiveness using multi-sensor remote sensing analysis. Integrated Sentinel-2A imagery, LiDAR topography, and spectral indices (NDVI/NDRE/NMCARI) with texture analysis to map 57% reduction in invasive coverage along Newmarket's Tom Taylor Trail. Developed a multi-threshold classification framework combining DEM analysis with variance/entropy texture metrics.

Key components: Change detection mapping • 10m resolution analysis • Multi-index thresholding • LiDAR-DEM integration • Urban wetland monitoring • Seasonal comparison (June-August 2024)

Polar Bears: Conservation & Social Impact - Climate Change Vulnerabilities and Human-Wildlife Dynamics
William Ross
EU/ENVS2420 M: Environmental Conservation, 2024
PDF / Population Statistics

Analyzed climate change impacts on 26,000 Canadian polar bears across 19 subpopulations, integrating GIS den mapping with traditional ecological knowledge. Revealed 30% projected population decline from sea-ice loss while evaluating socio-cultural impacts on Inuit communities and human-wildlife conflict case studies in Churchill, MB.

Key components: Arctic habitat vulnerability • Sea-ice decline analysis (-13%/decade) • Indigenous livelihood impacts • Population status tables • Denning GIS mapping • Human-bear conflict mitigation
